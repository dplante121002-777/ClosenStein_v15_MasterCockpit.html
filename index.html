SalesTool & Core Pitch Flow

This is your stick-and-move core. Follow the steps from top to bottom.

1) Pull Results & Send SalesTool Link

“Okay, I’ve pulled up your file here. First I’m going to verify a couple of details, then I’ll send you a secure link so you can see what I see on the screen.” • Verify address • Verify date of birth • Verify last 4 of SSN “Perfect, thank you. I’m going to place you on a very quick hold while I send a secure JG Wentworth link so you can see your credit report and the future savings estimates we’re talking about.” → Place on hold, send SalesTool invitation from Calculator tab (Email + Text). → Open the Hello screen, wait until you see their name. → Click the green button to connect.

2) Get Them Watching the Screen

“Okay, you should see my name and a JG Wentworth screen. Turn your phone sideways for me so it fills the whole screen. I’m only going to use two tabs with you: • Your credit report • Your future savings That way you actually see what I’m seeing instead of me just talking at you.”

3) No-Loan Frame + GREAT NEWS Pivot

“Looking at the numbers, you didn’t qualify for any **loan** options — mostly because of how high the utilization is on these cards. That’s actually what most people are running into right now. But I do have **good news**.” (Beat — let that land.) “What the approval **is** for is our **interest-free debt restructuring program**. Here is what that means in plain English: • We work directly with your creditors • We wipe away all the **future compounding interest** on these accounts • And based on how much interest you’ve already paid and the financial or medical hardship you mentioned, we’re often able to get a **discount off the principal balances** too. So instead of throwing almost 70% of your money at interest like you have been doing, you’re finally focusing on **paying down the actual balances**.”

4) Two Conditions (Cards + Credit Impact)

“Now, there are **two important conditions** you need to know up front so there are no surprises: 1) **No more charging on these cards** Because your creditors are going to be reducing the balances based on the percentage we negotiate, you can’t keep swiping the same cards in this program. Once your first program payment runs, those cards have to be treated like **bills**, not spending tools. You can use debit or a separate card that’s not in the plan for everyday stuff. 2) **Short-term credit impact** With debt resolution, don’t be surprised if there’s a little step back in your credit score at the beginning. It usually takes about **3–4 months** before we see the first resolutions hit the report. Those delinquencies are **temporary** — most clients recover in about **6 months** and then are in a better position because the **debt is under control**, not just hidden. Our focus is on your **debt-to-income ratio**, not a pretty number on the screen. Once the debt is under control, your overall financial life improves.”

5) Onemain / Late-Pay Question

“If you have an Onemain account, they normally respond within **30–45 days** from enrollment. Now, a lot of people ask about **late payments**. That’s a great question. This option is completely confidential on your credit report in the sense that it doesn’t show as a new loan. It’s done that way to protect your future borrowing options. Again — our focus is your **debt to income**, not the one-time score dip along the way. Any other questions before I go over the next steps?”

6) Expense Report → ACH Info → Close

“Let’s make sure this **actually works** with your real budget so you don’t get crushed a few months from now.” → Complete Expense/Budget in system. “Okay, based on that, the system is recommending the deposit we talked about. If that fits, the next step is simple — we set up your draft date and your dedicated account.” → Gather ACH info: • Bank name • Routing number (repeat back) • Account number (repeat back) • Confirm checking, not savings Then move to the Quote tab and read from the **Budget & Quote** section.

Post-Quote Checklist (1–6)

Use this AFTER they say the quote sounds good. Go straight down the list.

1) First Deposit / Pay Date

“Which paycheck feels safest for the first deposit?”
  • Confirm pay schedule (weekly / bi-weekly / semi-monthly / monthly)
  • Choose paycheck that feels safest
  • Set first draft date 7–14 days out
  • Repeat date and amount back to client

2) Banking Details

“Grab the checking account you use for bills so we can set up the dedicated account.”
  • Bank name
  • Routing number (repeat back)
  • Account number (repeat back)
  • Confirm checking (not savings)

3) State Recording

“Because you’re in [STATE], there’s a specific disclosure/recording I need to play so everything is 100% clear and compliant.”
  • Check state model (Standard / NSL / Arnold & Smith)
  • Play correct recording from system
  • For Florida – read FL block before sending agreement

4) Send Agreement

“I’m going to send the agreement while we’re on the phone so we can go through it together.”
  • Confirm email address (spell it back)
  • Confirm cell (if texting link)
  • Send agreement
  • Have them refresh inbox / check spam

5) Sign Together

“Open that email now and I’ll walk you through the key parts so you’re not guessing.”
  • Confirm they opened correct document
  • Highlight deposit, term, fee, cancel-anytime
  • Have them complete all signature/initial boxes
  • Confirm they clicked Finish / Submit

6) Explain What Happens Next

“Let me walk you through exactly what happens next so there are no surprises.”
  • Explain welcome/onboarding call timing
  • Reconfirm first draft amount + date
  • Set expectations about possible creditor calls
  • Mention portal / statements (if applicable)
  • Promise follow-up call after first draft
